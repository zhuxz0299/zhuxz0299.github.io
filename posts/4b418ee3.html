Negating a proposition

General rules:

  • ¬∀xϕ≡∃xϕ̸\lnot \forall x \phi \equiv \exists x \not \phi¬∀xϕ≡∃xϕ
  • ¬∃xϕ≡∀x¬ϕ\lnot \exists x \phi \equiv \forall x \lnot \phi¬∃xϕ≡∀x¬ϕ
  • ¬∀x(ϕ→ψ)≡∃x(ϕ∧¬ψ)\lnot \forall x(\phi \to \psi) \equiv \exists x(\phi \land \lnot \psi)¬∀x(ϕ→ψ)≡∃x(ϕ∧¬ψ)
  • ¬∃x(ϕ∧ψ)≡∀x(ϕ→¬ψ)\lnot \exists x(\phi \land \psi) \equiv \forall x(\phi \to \lnot \psi)¬∃x(ϕ∧ψ)≡∀x(ϕ→¬ψ)
  • ¬(ϕ∧ψ)≡¬ϕ∨¬ψ\lnot (\phi \land \psi) \equiv \lnot \phi \lor \lnot \psi¬(ϕ∧ψ)≡¬ϕ∨¬ψ
  • ¬(ϕ∨ψ)≡¬ϕ∧ψ\lnot (\phi \lor \psi)\equiv \lnot \phi \land \psi¬(ϕ∨ψ)≡¬ϕ∧ψ
  • ¬(ϕ→ψ)≡ϕ∧¬ψ\lnot (\phi\to \psi)\equiv \phi \land \lnot \psi¬(ϕ→ψ)≡ϕ∧¬ψ

Example
Proof of:

¬∃x∈N.∃y∈N.gcd⁡(x,y)=1 and x2=2y2.\lnot \exists x \in \mathbb{N}. \exists y \in \mathbb{N}. \gcd(x, y) = 1 \text{ and } x^2 = 2y^2. ¬∃x∈N.∃y∈N.gcd(x,y)=1 and x2=2y2.

Suppose there exists x∈Nx \in \mathbb{N}x∈N and y∈Ny \in \mathbb{N}y∈N s.t. x2=2y2x^2 = 2y^2x2=2y2.
Thus, xxx must be an even number and we can assume x=2x0x = 2x_0x=2x0​.
Now, we have y2=2x02y^2 = 2x_0^2y2=2x02​.
Thus, yyy must be an even number too and we can assume y=2y0y = 2y_0y=2y0​.
Therefore, gcd⁡(x,y)≠1\gcd(x, y) \neq 1gcd(x,y)=1.
Qed.

Proof step: In order to prove ¬∃x∈N.∃y∈N.gcd⁡(x,y)=1 and x2=2y2\lnot \exists x \in \mathbb{N}. \exists y \in \mathbb{N}. \gcd(x, y) = 1 \text{ and } x^2 = 2y^2¬∃x∈N.∃y∈N.gcd(x,y)=1 and x2=2y2, it suffices to prove that if x∈Nx \in \mathbb{N}x∈N, y∈Ny \in \mathbb{N}y∈N and x2=2y2x^2 = 2y^2x2=2y2 then gcd⁡(x,y)≠1\gcd(x, y) \neq 1gcd(x,y)=1.

Logic: The negation of ∃x(ψ1∧∃y(ψ2∧ψ3∧χ))\exists x (\psi_1 \land \exists y (\psi_2 \land \psi_3 \land \chi))∃x(ψ1​∧∃y(ψ2​∧ψ3​∧χ)) is ∀x(ψ1→∀y(ψ2→(ψ3→¬χ)))\forall x (\psi_1 \rightarrow \forall y (\psi_2 \rightarrow (\psi_3 \rightarrow \lnot \chi)))∀x(ψ1​→∀y(ψ2​→(ψ3​→¬χ))).

证明逻辑：

Φ⊢¬∃x(ψ1∧∃y(ψ2∧(ψ3∧χ)))  ⟸  Φ⊢∀x(ψ1→∀y(ψ2→(ψ3→¬χ)))  ⟸  Φ⊢ψ1→∀y(ψ2→(ψ3→¬χ))(x does not freely occur in Φ)  ⟸  Φ,ψ1⊢∀y(ψ2→(ψ3→¬χ))  ⟸  Φ,ψ1⊢ψ2→(ψ3→¬χ)(y does not freely occur in Φ,ψ1)  ⟸  Φ,ψ1,ψ2,ψ3⊢¬χ\begin{aligned} &\Phi\vdash\lnot \exists x(\psi_1\land\exists y(\psi_{2}\land(\psi_{3}\land\chi)))\\ \impliedby& \Phi\vdash\forall x(\psi_1\to \forall y (\psi_{2}\rightarrow(\psi_{3}\rightarrow \lnot \chi)))\\ \impliedby& \Phi\vdash \psi_1\to \forall y (\psi_{2}\rightarrow(\psi_{3}\rightarrow \lnot \chi)) \quad (x \text{ does not freely occur in } \Phi) \\ \impliedby&\Phi,\psi_{1}\vdash\forall y (\psi_{2}\rightarrow(\psi_{3}\rightarrow \lnot \chi))\\ \impliedby&\Phi,\psi_{1}\vdash \psi_{2}\rightarrow(\psi_{3}\rightarrow \lnot \chi) \quad (y \text{ does not freely occur in } \Phi, \psi_1) \\ \impliedby&\Phi,\psi_{1}, \psi_2, \psi_3 \vdash \lnot \chi \end{aligned} ⟸⟸⟸⟸⟸​Φ⊢¬∃x(ψ1​∧∃y(ψ2​∧(ψ3​∧χ)))Φ⊢∀x(ψ1​→∀y(ψ2​→(ψ3​→¬χ)))Φ⊢ψ1​→∀y(ψ2​→(ψ3​→¬χ))(x does not freely occur in Φ)Φ,ψ1​⊢∀y(ψ2​→(ψ3​→¬χ))Φ,ψ1​⊢ψ2​→(ψ3​→¬χ)(y does not freely occur in Φ,ψ1​)Φ,ψ1​,ψ2​,ψ3​⊢¬χ​

Important properties of logic equivalence and consequence relation

Theorem 4

  • ∀x (ϕ∧ψ)≡∀xϕ∧∀xψ\forall x\ (\phi \wedge \psi) \equiv \forall x\phi \wedge \forall x\psi∀x (ϕ∧ψ)≡∀xϕ∧∀xψ
  • ∀xϕ∨∀xψ⊨∀x (ϕ∨ψ)\forall x\phi \vee \forall x\psi \models \forall x\ (\phi \vee \psi)∀xϕ∨∀xψ⊨∀x (ϕ∨ψ)
  • ∃x (ϕ∨ψ)≡∃x ϕ∨∃x ψ\exists x\ (\phi \vee \psi) \equiv \exists x\ \phi \vee \exists x\ \psi∃x (ϕ∨ψ)≡∃x ϕ∨∃x ψ
  • ∃x (ϕ∧ψ)⊨∃x ϕ∧∃x ψ\exists x\ (\phi \wedge \psi) \models \exists x\ \phi \wedge \exists x\ \psi∃x (ϕ∧ψ)⊨∃x ϕ∧∃x ψ

  • If xxx does not freely occur in ϕ\phiϕ, ∀x(ϕ→ψ)≡ϕ→∀xψ\forall x(\phi \rightarrow \psi) \equiv \phi \rightarrow \forall x\psi∀x(ϕ→ψ)≡ϕ→∀xψ

Theorem 5

  • ∀x∀yϕ≡∀y∀xϕ\forall x\forall y\phi \equiv \forall y\forall x\phi∀x∀yϕ≡∀y∀xϕ
  • If xxx does not freely occur in ϕ2\phi_{2}ϕ2​ and yyy does not freely occur in ϕ1\phi_{1}ϕ1​, then ∀x(ϕ1→∀y(ϕ2→ψ))≡∀y(ϕ2→∀x(ϕ1→ψ))\forall x(\phi_{1} \rightarrow \forall y(\phi_{2} \rightarrow \psi)) \equiv \forall y(\phi_{2} \rightarrow \forall x(\phi_{1} \rightarrow \psi))∀x(ϕ1​→∀y(ϕ2​→ψ))≡∀y(ϕ2​→∀x(ϕ1​→ψ))
    Proof:

    ∀x(ϕ1→∀y(ϕ2→ψ))≡∀x∀y(ϕ1→(ϕ2→ψ))≡∀x∀y(ϕ1∧ϕ2→ψ)≡∀x∀y(ϕ2→(ϕ1→ψ))≡∀y∀x(ϕ2→(ϕ1→ψ))≡∀y(ϕ2→∀x(ϕ1→ψ))\begin{aligned} &\forall x(\phi_{1} \rightarrow \forall y(\phi_{2} \rightarrow \psi)) \\ \equiv & \forall x \forall y (\phi_1 \to (\phi_2 \to \psi)) \\ \equiv & \forall x\forall y(\phi_1 \land \phi_2\to \psi) \\ \equiv &\forall x\forall y(\phi_2\to (\phi_1\to \psi))\\ \equiv &\forall y\forall x(\phi_2\to (\phi_1\to \psi))\\ \equiv &\forall y(\phi_2\to \forall x(\phi_1\to \psi))\\ \end{aligned} ≡≡≡≡≡​∀x(ϕ1​→∀y(ϕ2​→ψ))∀x∀y(ϕ1​→(ϕ2​→ψ))∀x∀y(ϕ1​∧ϕ2​→ψ)∀x∀y(ϕ2​→(ϕ1​→ψ))∀y∀x(ϕ2​→(ϕ1​→ψ))∀y(ϕ2​→∀x(ϕ1​→ψ))​

  • If xxx does not freely occur in ϕ2\phi_{2}ϕ2​ and yyy does not freely occur in ϕ1\phi_{1}ϕ1​, then ∃x(ϕ1∧∃y(ϕ2∧ψ))≡∃y(ϕ2∧∃x(ϕ1∧ψ))\exists x(\phi_{1} \land \exists y(\phi_{2} \land \psi)) \equiv \exists y(\phi_{2} \land \exists x(\phi_{1} \land \psi))∃x(ϕ1​∧∃y(ϕ2​∧ψ))≡∃y(ϕ2​∧∃x(ϕ1​∧ψ))
  • ∃x∀yϕ⊨∀y∃xϕ\exists x\forall y\phi \models \forall y\exists x\phi∃x∀yϕ⊨∀y∃xϕ

Inference Rules and Proof Theory

Proof theory is an independent theory to the semantic theory.

Definition 6 (The natural deduction system). Φ⊢ψ\Phi \vdash \psiΦ⊢ψ (Φ\PhiΦ derives ψ\psiψ) if and only if it can be established by the following proof rules in finite steps:

  • ∀xϕ⊢ϕ[x↦t]\forall x \phi \vdash \phi[x \mapsto t]∀xϕ⊢ϕ[x↦t]; ϕ[x↦t]⊢∃xϕ\quad \phi[x \mapsto t] \vdash \exists x \phiϕ[x↦t]⊢∃xϕ;

  • If Φ⊢ψ\Phi \vdash \psiΦ⊢ψ and xxx does not freely occur in Φ\PhiΦ, then Φ⊢∀xψ\Phi \vdash \forall x \psiΦ⊢∀xψ.

  • If Φ,ψ⊢χ\Phi, \psi \vdash \chiΦ,ψ⊢χ and xxx does not freely occur in Φ\PhiΦ or χ\chiχ, then Φ,∃xψ⊢χ\Phi, \exists x \psi \vdash \chiΦ,∃xψ⊢χ.

  • ϕ,ψ⊢ϕ∧ψ\phi, \psi \vdash \phi \land \psiϕ,ψ⊢ϕ∧ψ; ϕ∧ψ⊢ϕ\quad \phi \land \psi \vdash \phiϕ∧ψ⊢ϕ; ϕ∧ψ⊢ψ\quad \phi \land \psi \vdash \psiϕ∧ψ⊢ψ

  • ϕ⊢ϕ∨ψ\phi \vdash \phi \lor \psiϕ⊢ϕ∨ψ; ψ⊢ϕ∨ψ\quad \psi \vdash \phi \lor \psiψ⊢ϕ∨ψ

  • If Φ,ϕ1⊢ψ\Phi, \phi_1 \vdash \psiΦ,ϕ1​⊢ψ and Φ,ϕ2⊢ψ\Phi, \phi_2 \vdash \psiΦ,ϕ2​⊢ψ, then Φ,ϕ1∨ϕ2⊢ψ\Phi, \phi_1 \lor \phi_2 \vdash \psiΦ,ϕ1​∨ϕ2​⊢ψ

  • Φ⊢ψ∨¬ψ\Phi \vdash \psi \lor \neg \psiΦ⊢ψ∨¬ψ

  • Φ,ψ,¬ψ⊢χ\Phi, \psi, \neg \psi \vdash \chiΦ,ψ,¬ψ⊢χ

  • If ϕ∈Φ\phi \in \Phiϕ∈Φ, then Φ⊢ϕ\Phi \vdash \phiΦ⊢ϕ

  • If Φ⊆Ψ\Phi \subseteq \PsiΦ⊆Ψ and Φ⊢ϕ\Phi \vdash \phiΦ⊢ϕ, then Ψ⊢ϕ\Psi \vdash \phiΨ⊢ϕ

  • If Φ⊢ψ\Phi \vdash \psiΦ⊢ψ and Φ⊢ψ→χ\Phi \vdash \psi \rightarrow \chiΦ⊢ψ→χ, then Φ⊢χ\Phi \vdash \chiΦ⊢χ.

  • If Φ,ψ⊢χ\Phi, \psi \vdash \chiΦ,ψ⊢χ, then Φ⊢ψ→χ\Phi \vdash \psi \rightarrow \chiΦ⊢ψ→χ.
